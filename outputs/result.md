# NIST Artificial Intelligence Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0)

## Summary
This document is NIST AI 100-1, the Artificial Intelligence Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0), published in January 2023 by the U.S. Department of Commerce under Secretary Gina M. Raimondo and the National Institute of Standards and Technology under Director Laurie E. Locascio. The publication is available free of charge at https://doi.org/10.6028/NIST.AI.100-1. The AI RMF is designed as a living document intended to be regularly reviewed and updated by NIST, with a formal review from the AI community expected no later than 2028. The framework employs a two-number versioning system where the first number represents the generation (e.g., 1.0) and changes only with major revisions, while minor revisions are tracked with '.n' notation (e.g., 1.1). Comments on the AI RMF Playbook may be sent via email to AIframework@nist.gov and will be reviewed and integrated on a semi-annual basis. The framework addresses AI risk management across multiple dimensions including trustworthiness attributes (valid and reliable, safe, secure and resilient, accountable and transparent, explainable and interpretable, privacy-enhanced, and fair with managed harmful bias). The document is organized into foundational information covering risk framing, audience, AI risks and trustworthiness, and effectiveness, followed by core functions and profiles including Govern, Map, Measure, and Manage functions.

## Key Points
- The AI RMF is intended to be a living document with regular reviews and updates
  - Evidence: The Artificial Intelligence Risk Management Framework (AI RMF) is intended to be a living document. NIST will review the content and usefulness of the Framework regularly to determine if an update is appropriate; a review with formal input from the AI community is expected to take place no later than 2028.
- The framework uses a two-number versioning system to track major and minor changes
  - Evidence: The Framework will employ a two-number versioning system to track and identify major and minor changes. The first number will represent the generation of the AI RMF and its companion documents (e.g., 1.0) and will change only with major revisions. Minor revisions will be tracked using '.n' after the generation number (e.g., 1.1).
- The framework addresses seven key trustworthiness attributes for AI systems
  - Evidence: Valid and Reliable, Safe, Secure and Resilient, Accountable and Transparent, Explainable and Interpretable, Privacy-Enhanced, Fair – with Harmful Bias Managed
- Community feedback on the AI RMF Playbook is solicited and integrated regularly
  - Evidence: Comments on the AI RMF Playbook may be sent via email to AIframework@nist.gov at any time and will be reviewed and integrated on a semi-annual basis.
- The framework includes four core functions for AI risk management
  - Evidence: AI RMF Core includes Govern, Map, Measure, and Manage functions

## Risks
- Risk measurement challenges in AI systems
- Risk tolerance determination difficulties
- Risk prioritization complexities
- Organizational integration and management of AI risk
- Potential harms related to AI systems including bias and fairness issues
- Security and resilience vulnerabilities in AI systems
- Privacy concerns in AI applications
- Lack of explainability and interpretability in AI systems
- Accountability and transparency gaps in AI deployment
